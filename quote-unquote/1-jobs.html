Quote Unquote · Edition 1

Making Something Wonderful

Steve Jobs on the best way to show appreciation for humanity.

By Nate

Welcome to the first edition of Quote Unquote — a weekly exploration of quotes that deserve more than a quick scroll.

Each week, I'll share a quote that's stuck with me, tell you the story behind it, and explore why it still matters. Think of it as a few minutes to slow down and actually think about words that deserve thinking about.

Let's start with the quote that motivated me to build an app and start this newsletter:

"There's lots of ways to be, as a person. And some people express their deep appreciation in different ways. But one of the ways that I believe people express their appreciation to the rest of humanity is to make something wonderful and put it out there." — Steve Jobs

The Story Behind It

Steve Jobs said this at an internal Apple meeting on October 23, 2007. It had been a remarkable year. In January, Apple dropped "Computer" from its name, signaling a broader vision. In June, the iPhone debuted to lines around the block. Revenues were up, profits were way up, and Apple was on top of the world.

During the Q&A, an employee asked how Apple would keep its culture intact as it grew. Jobs paused, took a sip of water, and gave an answer that cut straight to what mattered.

He talked about appreciation.

Not the kind you express with a thank-you note. Something deeper. He believed that making something wonderful—pouring care and craft into your work—was itself an act of gratitude. A way of saying thank you to everyone who came before you.

Think about that for a second.

Every tool you use, every piece of software, every app on your phone—someone made that. They spent late nights debugging code, redesigning interfaces, rethinking how things work. Not (just) for profit. But as their way of contributing something worthwhile to the world.

Jobs continued: "And you never meet the people. You never shake their hands. You never hear their story or tell yours. But somehow, in the act of making something with a great deal of care and love, something's transmitted there. And it's a way of expressing to the rest of our species our deep appreciation."

Creation is an act of service. A way to give back to all the people whose work made your life possible.

Why It Matters Today

We live in an era where everyone's a creator—or at least, everyone's told they should be.

Start a business. Launch a YouTube channel. Become a social media influencer. The pressure to make something, anything, is everywhere. However, a lot of it feels… hollow. Motivated by hustle culture, by the need to have a "side project," by the fear of being left behind.

But this quote cuts through all that noise.

It reframes creation as gratitude.

Not: "I need to build something to prove I'm valuable."
But: "I want to make something wonderful as my way of saying thank you."

That shift in perspective changes the motivation.

It means you don't have to create content for the algorithm. You don't have to chase virality or trends. You don't have to build something just because everyone else is.

You create because you have something worth sharing. Because you've been shaped by the books you've read, the tools you've used, the people who've taught you – and this is how you pass it forward.

Making something wonderful isn't about ego. It's about generosity.

And the best part is it doesn't have to be something big.

A carefully designed spreadsheet that saves someone hours? That's wonderful.
A thoughtfully written email that helps someone see things differently? That's wonderful.
An app that captures quotes so people don't lose the words that shaped them? That's wonderful too.

Wonderful also doesn't mean perfect. It means made with care. Made with the intent to be useful, beautiful, or meaningful to someone else.

That's why I built Quips. Not because the world needs another app, but because I wanted a place dedicated to keeping the quotes that mattered to me. And if this app solves that problem for me, then maybe it can help others remember the words that matter to them.

Not world-changing. Just… hopefully, wonderful.

Related Quotes

If this idea resonates, here are a few more quotes that explore the same theme:

On Creating as Service:

"The purpose of life is to discover your gift; the work of life is to develop it; and the meaning of life is to give your gift away." — David Viscott

This comes from Viscott's 1993 book Finding Your Strength in Difficult Times. What you make is how you give.

On Making Things for Others:

"We are cups, constantly and quietly being filled. The trick is knowing how to tip ourselves over and let the beautiful stuff out." — Ray Bradbury, Zen in the Art of Writing

Bradbury understood that creation isn't about emptying yourself—it's about overflowing with what you've absorbed and letting it spill out for others.
